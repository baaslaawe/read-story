The Crab


Saturday was supposed to be a gloomy day, but when I woke up the sun was shining and there wasn’t a cloud in the sky. “What a great day to go the beach,” I thought. I invited Rebecca to come with me, but instead of being ecstatic about going to the beach, she was unenthusiastic and decided to stay home. Next, I called my cousin Miguel, who was eager to go to the beach.


We arrived at the beach and laid out our towels and beach chairs. Miguel saw a crab in the sand and he walked over to it. I thought it would scurry away, but the crab stayed where it was. It wasn’t afraid of Miguel at all! At first I pretended I wasn’t interested, but I was curious about the crab. Finally, I walked over to Miguel and the crab. “Miguel, that crab is sick. It needs to go back into the water. You need to pick it up and take it into the ocean right now!”


I thought that Miguel would complain about having to pick up the crab, but he happily took the crab back down to the ocean. We both watched the crab to see what would happen. I was not sure if the crab would be okay. I tried to relax, but I was feeling very anxious. Miguel told me that although this was a new thing for me, he was very familiar with crabs and he was not worried about the crab at all. He told me to stop being so impatient. The crab just needed some time to feel better before he swam away.


Miguel was right! After a few minutes, the tiny crab began to swim away. I was delighted! This turned out to be a splendid day at the beach instead of a boring day at home.
