High-Flying Dreams



1 Jacqueline Cochran was born in a small town in Florida around 1910. Her parents died when she was young, and she had to work from early on. For years she dreamed of starting her own business one day. It was a big dream, but she made that dream come true. Along the way, Jackie Cochran also became one of the greatest fliers of all time.



2 As a young woman, Jackie Cochran worked in a beauty shop in Florida. In 1929, she moved to New York City and got a lucky break. She got a job in a beauty shop on Fifth Avenue, the center of fashion at that time. She had many rich customers. Some of them hired her to travel with them. She began to think of starting a business selling makeup and beauty products.



3 On one trip, Jackie met a rich man named Floyd Odlum. She told him about her dream. He advised her to learn how to fly an airplane to help her in business. She would need to move quickly from city to city.



4 Jackie quickly learned to fly and fell in love with flying. Starting a business could wait. Soon Jackie was racing airplanes.



5 Jackie had some bad luck in her first three years as a racer. Her planes kept breaking down. But in 1937, she won her first cross-country race. She was picked as the top woman flier of the year. By then, she was also running her own business selling makeup.



6 During World War II, Jackie joined the Army Air Corps. She tested planes and flew them overseas. Jackie won a prize for her leadership.



7 After the war, Jackie returned to racing planes. Later she tested jets, and she ran her business. During the 1950s, she was twice voted “Woman of the Year in Business.”


8 Jackie Cochran died in 1980. She set many flying records in her lifetime and won many honors. Best of all, perhaps, she lived her dream.
